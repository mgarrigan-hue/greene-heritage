The Greene Family

Tracing the Greene lineage from Portarlington to Dublin, the Channel Islands, and beyond

Our Story

The Greene family's roots run deep in the midlands of Ireland, in the market town of Portarlington — a place that straddles two counties, King's County (now Offaly) and Queen's County (now Laois), divided by the River Barrow.

From there, Thomas Greene (born c.1871) made his way to Dublin, where he built a life as a Shop Porter and later a Gentleman's Club Steward. He married twice, raised seven children, and served in the British Army. His daughter Elizabeth would go on to live one of the most remarkable stories in the family — interned by the Nazis in a German camp during World War II, where she served as a nurse caring for fellow prisoners.

This site is a living research project, documenting what we've discovered so far and preserving it for future generations.
