A genealogy without sources is just a story. This page lists every record, document, and conversation that underpins the people, events, and relationships displayed across the site. Where a record is held in an online archive, a link is provided. Where the source is family oral history, that is stated honestly. Confidence levels follow the genealogical standard: primary sources (original document, contemporaneous), high (multiple corroborating records), medium (one strong record, plausible inference), low (oral history or single weak inference).

Research Log — Negative Evidence

A list of searches we have already run that returned no result. Recording these saves future researchers from repeating dead-end work.

Thomas × Bridget Marriage Record (Dublin, c.1915–1925)

Not yet found. IrishGenealogy.ie civil marriage indexes for "Thomas Greene" + "Bridget Clarke" across all Dublin districts 1915–1925 returned no exact match in spring 2026. Variant spellings (Green / Clark) also tried. Possible the marriage was registered in a neighbouring county or under a slightly different name. This is the highest-priority outstanding record — it would name Bridget's father.

Catherine Clarke's Husband (Bridget's Father)

Identity unknown. Catherine Clarke was already a widow by the 1901 Census. His death predates 1901 civil registration searches we have completed for Mullaghfin / Duleek. The marriage record between Catherine and Mr Clarke (c.1875–1885) remains unfound. Will be resolved by either: (a) finding their marriage record, or (b) finding Bridget's birth registration which would name him.

Thomas Greene's First Marriage (Dublin South 1899)

Indexed but image not yet retrieved. Ancestry.com index entry: Dublin South Registration District, 1899, Vol 2, Page 593. The actual register image is on IrishGenealogy.ie and would name Elizabeth's maiden surname and Thomas's father — closing two open questions in one document.

Elizabeth Harris (née Greene) — Channel Islands Records

Pivot from Guernsey → Jersey. Until April 2026 we assumed Elizabeth was on Guernsey. The newly-surfaced 1941 Jersey marriage certificate confirms she was in fact in Jersey, parish of St. Saviour's. All future Channel Islands research should target Jersey Archive, not Guernsey. Specifically: German Occupation Registration Cards (with photographs) for both Alfred and Elizabeth Harris.

Thomas Greene — Military Service Record

Not yet retrieved. Service papers for the Prince of Wales's Leinster Regiment (Royal Canadians) are held at The National Archives, Kew (WO 363 / WO 364) and on Findmypast.co.uk. Many WWI service records were destroyed in the 1940 Blitz ("burnt documents"); those that survive are paywalled. Requires a Findmypast subscription or Kew visit.

Methodology Notes

  • Census triangulation: Where a person appears in 1901, 1911, and 1926 census, ages are cross-checked. A discrepancy of ±1 year is treated as normal (different enumeration months); > 3 years requires a research note.
  • Birthplace ambiguity: Portarlington straddles the historic county line between Kings Co. (Offaly) and Queens Co. (Laois). Thomas Greene is recorded under both at different censuses — both are correct depending on which side of the river the household stood.
  • Name normalisation: "Greene" / "Green" are treated as the same surname; the family used both spellings interchangeably until c.1900 after which "Greene" predominates. "Eithne" / "Ita" / "Eita" are all the same person — Mark's grandmother-in-law.
  • 1926 Census: Released on 18 April 2026 — exactly 100 years after enumeration day — under Ireland's century rule. Several of this site's most important findings (Bridget's Duleek birthplace, Thomas at Switzers) emerged on launch day.
  • Confidence inheritance: A parent-child relationship can never have higher confidence than the lower of the two people's records. The site's family.json records confidence on every relationship explicitly.
  • Living people: Birth dates and addresses for living people (Mark, Claire, Donnelly cousins) are deliberately omitted in line with normal genealogical practice.
